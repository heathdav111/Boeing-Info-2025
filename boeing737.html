Boeing 737

The Boeing 737 is the world’s most successful commercial jet aircraft. Designed for short- to medium-haul routes, it is widely used by airlines around the globe for its efficiency, reliability, and versatility.

Quick Facts

  • First flight: 1967
  • Typical seating: 126–220 passengers
  • Range: ~3,500 nautical miles
  • Engines: CFM International

Engines

The Boeing 737 is powered by CFM International turbofan engines, designed for fuel efficiency, reduced noise, and long-term reliability. They generate 27,000 pounnds of thrust

Wings

The swept wings provide aerodynamic efficiency and are often equipped with winglets to reduce drag and improve fuel economy.

Landing Gear

The reinforced landing gear allows the 737 to safely operate from shorter runways commonly used at regional airports.

Cockpit

The cockpit features modern avionics, glass displays, and advanced flight management systems to assist pilots during all phases of flight.
